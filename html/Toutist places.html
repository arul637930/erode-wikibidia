Erode Museum

Erode Government Museum will appeal to culture buffs and history lovers. The museum is a rich repository of objects related to art, anthropology and archeology, being one of the largest storehouses of art and culture in Tamil Nadu. The museum was established in 1987 and is open on Sundays too.

Government Museum is the prominent gallery that exhibits regional art and traditions. The museum was opened for public in 1987, displaying inscriptions belonging to the Kongu Chola Kingdom. Artifacts, handicrafts and stones from Bargur are some of the major attractions at the gallery. Furthermore, there are separate sections to display Thanjore paintings, manuscripts and other pre-history items.

Besides, the museum is known for housing botanical and zoological specimens along with an extensive collection of palm-leaf manuscripts.

Erode Bird Sanctuary

Birds Sanctuary, Vellode is about 15 kms from Erode. It is at a large lake surrounded with semi-dark bushes near Vellode. This .772 km2 (0.298 sq mi) sanctuary is home to many foreign birds. The sanctuary features thousands of birds coming from various countries, some of which can be easily identified. Some easily found bird species include cormorants, teals, pintail ducks, pelicans, and darters.

Bhavanisagar Dam

Bhavanisagar Dam or Lower Bhavani Dam, is located in Erode district, Tamil Nadu, India. The dam is constructed on the Bhavani river.It is one of the world's largest earthen dams. The dam is situated some 16 km (9.9 mi) west of Sathyamangalam, is 17 km (11 mi) North of Punjai Puliampatti, is 35 km (22 mi) from Gobichettipalayam and is 36 km (22 mi) north-east to Mettupalayam.

Bhavanisagar Dam is about 16 kms. from Sathyamangalam across the river Bhavani.Kodiveri Dam is about 10 kms from Gobichettipalayam and 55 kms from Erode.

Kodiveri Dam

Discover a picturesque dam and waterfall nestled in the scenic Erode district near Satyamangalam, just a short distance from Coimbatore and Erode. This popular tourist spot not only boasts of stunning views but also provides water for irrigation to over 25000 hectares of land. At the base of the dam, a lively park awaits with an array of thrilling attractions, rides, and games for visitors to enjoy. Come and experience the beauty and excitement of this must-see destination.

Kongalvan Vettuva Gounder King built the dam in the year 1125 AD. A 20-foot rock wall was carved out in order to build the dam. Iron bars were then used to interlock the stones, and lead was used as mortar. The small waterfall created by the flowing river water from the other side of the dam is purely a delight to the eyes.

As it is not particularly heavy, the bubbling, flowing water falls to the earth gently. A dip in the falls is safe, and you can even go swimming here, which would be a memorable experience. Coracle rowers sell freshly caught fish which you can buy and enjoy cooking live. The entry fee costs only Rs.5 per person. Both government-allocated car parking and private car parking are available at Rs.20 and Rs.30 respectively. In the upper part of the dam, coracle rides are provided for Rs.50 per person for a roundtrip.

Kodiveri Dam is well-connected by road. You can take a bus from Erode, Coimbatore or nearby towns to reach the dam.

Sathyamangalam Tiger Reserve

A reserve forest, the Sathyamangalam Tiger Reserve is one of the most popular sightseeing places in Erode. Located at the juncture of the Western and the Eastern Ghats, the forest reserve was declared a sanctuary in 2011. The topography supports a rich fauna. Travellers can spot Indian elephants, gaurs, wild boars, leopards, barking deer, hyenas and antelopes. Bird watchers can delight spotting bulbuls, blabbers, mynahs, treepies and even vultures.

Karadiyur View point

Hiking in the Karadiyur view point is a fun thing to do in Erode. Karadiyur View Point is a popular tourist destination in the Erode district of Tamil Nadu, located on the banks of the Kaveri River and offers stunning views of the river valley. This place is located at a distance of about 2 km from the city centre and offers a panoramic view of the city. The view point is situated at an altitude of about 1400 metres and is perfect for enjoying the natural beauty of the place. Stay longer or reach little earlier if you are a fan of the sunrise and sunset as this place is the ideal spot of watching the sunrise and sunset.
